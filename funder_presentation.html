The Measurement Gap

DAs are among the most powerful actors in the criminal justice system ‚Äî yet we have no systematic data on what their policies actually say.

What Exists Today

  • Case outcome data (too downstream)
  • Campaign rhetoric (often dishonest)
  • Media coverage (filtered, biased)
  • Anecdotal comparisons

What's Missing

  • Actual internal policy documents
  • Systematic coding across offices
  • Temporal tracking of policy change
  • Linkage to outcomes for causal ID
Our contribution: The first large-scale, AI-coded dataset of internal DA policy documents ‚Äî creating infrastructure for rigorous causal inference.

The Data: A Unique Natural Resource

California's Racial Justice Act (AB 2542) required DA offices to produce internal policy and training documents via public records requests. The ACLU of Northern California collected and published these.

What We Learned: Honesty About Causality

‚úì What Holds Up

  • Cross-sectional correlations survive year controls (r=-0.22, p=0.009)
  • Progressive counties jail 68.5 fewer per 100k (d=-0.81)
  • Pretrial detention shows cleanest causal signal (pre-trend p=0.90)

‚úó What Doesn't

  • Naive Gasc√≥n pre/post is confounded by COVID ‚Äî all CA counties declined
  • Parallel trends violated for jail pop rate (p=0.021)
  • TWFE non-significant (N=137) ‚Äî need more power
The causal question remains open: Do progressive policies cause lower incarceration, or do progressive DAs get elected in places that were already declining? Answering this requires better identification, more data, or both. The infrastructure to answer it is ready.

AI Coding Methodology

How It Works

  • Each PDF extracted ‚Üí sent to Claude (Anthropic) with structured prompt
  • Returns JSON with 30+ coded variables per document
  • Temperature = 0.1 for consistency
  • Automatic rate limiting, checkpointing, error handling
  • Cost: ~$0.03/document ($80 total)

Validation Strategy

  • Face validity on 30 random documents
  • Known-case validation (SF/LA under progressive DAs vs. traditional)
  • Distribution checks for coding consistency
  • Proposed: 200-doc dual-coding by law students for gold-standard IRR
Anticipated pushback: "Can you trust AI coding?" We treat it as a first-pass instrument. The human validation study (Phase 1, ~$20k) directly addresses this. Known-case validation is already encouraging ‚Äî Gasc√≥n/Boudin offices code as expected.

Anticipated Questions & Responses

"Policy documents ‚â† practice. How do you know what's written actually happens?"

You're right ‚Äî that's exactly why Avenue 1 (outcome linkage) is Priority 1. We test whether "diversion-positive" memos predict more diversions. Avenue 3 includes DA interviews to validate. But note: even the stated-policy variation we found is itself a novel descriptive contribution ‚Äî nobody has systematically measured this before.

"AI coding seems unreliable. How do you validate it?"

Phase 1 includes a 200-document dual-coding study with trained law students (~$20k). Known-case validation already works: Gasc√≥n/Boudin offices score as expected. The AI is a first-pass instrument ‚Äî the validation study calibrates and benchmarks it. LLM-based coding is increasingly accepted in political science (see Argyle et al. 2023, Ornstein et al. 2024).

"You only have California. Isn't this just a Blue State story?"

That's the point of Avenue 2. CA provides internal variation (progressive Bay Area vs. traditional Central Valley), but external validity requires multi-state expansion. We specifically target TX, FL, PA to include red/purple jurisdictions. The pipeline already works ‚Äî scaling to new states is the marginal cost, not the fixed cost.

"Your pilot DiD didn't work. Why should I fund more?"

The pilot DiD did work for pretrial detention (pre-trend p=0.90). The honest reporting of failures is the feature, not the bug ‚Äî it showed exactly what's needed (more power, better controls). Cross-sectional correlations are robust (p=0.009 with year controls). The funded extensions address each limitation the pilot identified.
